Northstar Photonics

My First Internship!

Northstar Photonics, a company with fewer than 50 employees creating cutting-edge technology, has provided me with an incredible internship experience thus far (as of 7/1/24). Being their first intern and the youngest by more than two decades, I initially felt quite nervous.

I joined NSP as a supply chain procurement intern, in my first week I was immersed in the field of photonics, working on a new project with numerous moving parts. During this time, I gained extensive knowledge and was deeply involved in creating bills of materials, workflow charts, planning project schedules and facilitating communication across all departments, it felt amazing to actually contribute to something using the exact skills I had been learning in the previous semester!

I was able to sit in on executive meetings with my manager, observing how high level managers plan, track and execute, this was invaluable and has been one of the biggest learning experiences to date. The dedication to communication, relationship building, leadership and curiosity was exemplified in every meeting I attended, highlighting how vital these skills are across all industries.

I learn something new everyday, whether it's presenting to the CEO/COO on a project or identifying and solving issues with vendors to maintain production schedules, this experience has provided me with knowledge that is not obtainable in the classroom and I am greatful everyday to be apart of such a fun, innovative and creative company.

Salesforce Lab Assistant

Who knew I would be a TA!

During my Spring 2024 semester, I took BUS4 110b (Fundamentals of MIS) with Professor Timothy Hill. Initially, the class focused heavily on Python and Pandas, which I didn't particularly enjoy. While I understand the concepts and can code decently, I prefer a mix of solitary work and team interaction over coding all day. In the first half of the class, I didn't interact much with Professor Hill, only answering questions occasionally. However, one day, I decided to talk to him after class, and that conversation changed everything. Motivated by his quirky anecdotes and genuine personality, I began excelling in his class. I started attending his office hours regularly, seeking advice on projects and interview prep. During one of our conversations, Professor Hill told me about a role as a Salesforce Lab Assistant in the MIS department, which was perfect timing as many previous assistants were leaving. I accepted the role and was tasked with auditing six combined labs, each with over 100 steps. Over the summer, I meticulously ensured all directions were clear and straightforward for non-MIS students learning Salesforce. I continue to work in this position, enjoying the opportunity to support non-techy students in learning a new skill while gaining valuable experience with Salesforce.
